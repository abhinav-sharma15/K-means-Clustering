Calculating predictions with clustering in R

For this project, I am a hypothetical data analyst for a large wholesale company that supplies various retailers with wine. My company is known for its high-quality wines. However, maintaining the high standard by only stocking wines that fulfil a certain quality standard can be a tedious and expensive process that requires the input of wine experts. To overcome this limitation, I plan to use a clustering model to group similar wines from previous years together, and then use these groups to categorise new wines based on physicochemical properties that are prominent in high-quality wines. Using these groups, I aim to determine whether a wine contains the physicochemical properties of a high-quality wine before stocking this wine. I also aim to build a clustering model that is able to identify types of wine, red or white, based on its physicochemical properties. Besides the hypothetical business problem, my aim is also to practically understand difference between k-means and hierarchical clustering and what situations favor each.

Description of the data

This data set contains a total of 4,898 white and 1,599 red Vinho Verde wine samples from the north of Portugal. The composition of the data set is therefore 75% white and 25% red wine samples. Each sample was tested by wine experts and scored between 0 and 10, with 0 representing a very bad wine and 10 representing an excellent wine. For privacy and logistical reasons, only physicochemical and sensory variables are available. No data regarding the grape types, wine brand, or price is available.

for(i in 2:20) {

  silhouette_vec[i] <- mean(ss[,3])

}

Conclusion

What can we infer or predict from the analysis?

From the analysis that we did using scatterplot of quality against other variables, we observed that alcohol content has some correlation with quality. However, no variable showed deterministic influence on quality. What we essentially want here is more dots in the circled region indicating some relation between higher quality and alcohol content. We see this here in a limited but nondeterministic way. For other variables, there is no condensed pattern whatsoever in any region of the plot (linear upward or downward) and the quality is almost evenly spread across the middle axis. There is no bias noticed for a particular kind of wine as well (Red or white). A distinct difference in measure is seen for total sulphur dioxide content for red vs white wine. From the plots it can be said that red wine has lower total.sulfur.dioxide content compared to white wine. We saw difference in chloride levels as well, however the range in this case is very small (two consecutive peaks of averages) hence not conclusive in determining difference between red and white wine.

How can a new wine with avaialble physicochemical properties be analyzed based on above model?

From the k-means clustering execution, we can infer that cluster 3 has best average quality at 6.14. This is followed by cluster 2 (5.58) and finally cluster 1 (5.54). Although the difference is not broad, but we can get some understanding of influential factors if we analyse traits of these 3 clusters. Some observations that can help us analyse a newly launched wine –

  • Cluster 3 with highest average quality has the highest alcohol content as well. It is followed by cluster 2 and 1. This indicates positive correlation of alcohol content with quality. So one hypothesis we can carry forward is that more alcohol content is a indicator of better quality. Alcohol content is perhaps the only parameter that we can judge independent of wine colour.

  • Total sulphur dioxide is least for cluster 2 at 52.51 followed by cluster 3 (117.77) and then cluster 1 (168.17). although this is not in line with quality rankings but on observing the chart, we can infer that this parameter is a dependent of wine colour. Total sulphur dioxide will be more for white wine and less for red wines. Hence, while looking at our k-means clusters, this comparison is really between cluster 1 and 3 since both are dominated by white wine samples. From here we can deduce that lower content is generally an indicator of better quality. Therefore, Analysis of a new wine would depend on its wine colour.

  • Apart from the above two, we can also say that there is a negative correlation between wine quality and residual sugar content. Less sugar content is an indicator of a quality wine.

How is hierarchical clustering different from k-means

Hierarchical clustering showed more refined separation from the point of view of parameter total.sulfur.dioxide. There are 3 almost distinct sections based on measure of this parameter with very little interference. Following differences were observed – K means clustering was more spherical in nature with data points mixing to some extent. However, hierarchical clustering tended to slice the data points in to 3 regions based on total.sulfur.dioxide values. The approach for hierarchical clustering makes it prone to outliers and noise. If there are outliers present, the concerned cluster could be smaller in size compared to others. We see that for cluster 3 in our hierarchical cluster. Moreover, hierarchical clustering does a better job of classifying red vs white wine based on sulphur dioxide content. Hence, based on the requirements, either k-means or hierarchical clustering could be a preferred mode of clustering here.
